What is a Rainforest?

Rainforests are dense forests found in tropical regions with high annual rainfall. They cover approximately 6% of Earth's surface but are home to more than 50% of all plant and animal species.

Why Are Rainforests Important?

Rainforests play a crucial role in regulating the global climate, producing oxygen, absorbing carbon dioxide, and providing habitats for a vast number of species. Additionally, rainforests are a source of medicine, food, and resources for people around the world.

What Threatens Rainforests?

Rainforests are under constant threat from deforestation, illegal logging, mining, agriculture, and climate change. These activities are leading to habitat loss, biodiversity decline, and increasing global temperatures.
